Frequently Asked Questions

What can I do as an Open Humans member?

You can start right away by joining some of the studies we're partnered with. Once you're enrolled, you'll be able to connect that account and earn a badge on your profile.

As a member, you can look forward to new opportunities to donate your data to researchers – and receive access to new data in return!

What studies can I currently join?

We’re opening with three research partners:

  • American Gut - learn about the bacteria that live on you and inside you
  • GoViral - take samples when you get sick to find out what virus you had
  • Harvard Personal Genome Project - sequencing and publicly sharing whole genome data

Check out our activities page to learn more about participating in these studies.

I'm a researcher. Can I get my research study added to your site?

We'd love to expand our research partners! Learn how to connect your research study if you're interested in working with Open Humans members and data.

What is your "public data sharing" option?

We want to give people the opportunity to publicly share data research studies generate. Public data can be a great good for society. In many cases, these data wouldn't otherwise be publicly shared due to privacy concerns – but privacy preferences vary. While some participants prefer privacy, others may be happy to see their data be a public resource.

How do I make my data public?

If you enroll in our Public Data Sharing study, you'll have the ability to activate public sharing for any of your data files. Public sharing is always under your control, you choose which files to share and can turn it off at any time.

To learn more you can read the study's research subject information and consent form (PDF). If you enroll, we'll ask you to review that document and take a brief enrollment quiz. Click here to start the process!

I have other data I want to donate!

We're going to continue to expand ways people can contribute. In particular, we're looking into adding connections to APIs from various sources to enable you to import personal data. The data you store – from studies and personal sources – can become a valuable resource used by future research studies.

How can I find out when you have new studies or features?

Our newsletter will be an important way we alert members to new opportunities and features. If you're interested in receiving notifications about new studies and other Open Humans updates, make sure you're signed up (see Profile Settings).

What does Open Humans do to keep my data secure?

We take data security seriously. In addition to ensuring we use secure methods for our website and data, we've developed a set of security guidelines we expect our research partner websites to follow.

If you have a security concern to report to Open Humans, please let us know at security@openhumans.org .

Can I contact an Open Humans member?

We will soon enable the ability for Open Humans members to contact each other. Members can choose whether or not to receive messages from other members (see Profile Settings). To send a message to an Open Humans member, you must be a member yourself.

Why doesn't Open Humans require members to be 18 or older?

The Open Humans site itself is not a study, but a community. Our Terms of Use has a lower age requirement (13 years) because we want Open Humans to be a place youth can explore and learn about research studies and resulting data.

The research studies our site connects to typically require participants be 18 years of age, but there will be other ways Members can interact on the site. These could include participating in shared discussions, accessing data, and sending messages to other Members.
